⚠️ Payment Security Tips Read before paying
1Use a fresh wallet address every order ▼
Never reuse a Bitcoin address across orders. Generate a new receiving address each time — this breaks the transaction chain so no one can link your purchases.
2Don't buy BTC from a KYC exchange ▼
Exchanges like Coinbase or Binance tie your identity to your wallet. Instead use a Bitcoin ATM with cash (no ID under certain limits) or peer-to-peer platforms like Bisq that require no KYC.
3Consider Monero (XMR) for full privacy ▼
Bitcoin transactions are public. Monero hides sender, receiver and amount by default. Ask us about XMR — we accept it.
4Use a Bitcoin mixer before sending ▼
If your BTC is tied to your identity, run it through a mixer (e.g., Wasabi Wallet's CoinJoin) before sending to break the trace.
5Use Tor or a VPN when transacting ▼
Your IP can be logged when broadcasting a Bitcoin transaction. Use Tor Browser or a trusted no-log VPN before accessing your wallet or this site.
1Buy with physical cash at a store ▼
Walk into Walmart, CVS, Walgreens, or any convenience store and buy Amazon, Google Play, Steam, or iTunes gift cards with cash. Zero digital trace.
2Accepted cards and minimum amounts ▼
We accept: Amazon, Google Play, Steam Wallet, iTunes/Apple, and Visa/Mastercard prepaid gift cards. Minimum $50 per card. Send code + PIN via Telegram — never by email.
3Don't buy online with a linked card ▼
Buying gift cards online with your debit or credit card creates a paper trail. Cash-purchased physical cards are untraceable.
4Activate on mobile data, not home WiFi ▼
If a card requires online activation, use mobile data or a coffee shop WiFi — not your home network, which has a fixed IP registered to your name.
5Photograph and send via Telegram only ▼
Scratch the code, photograph it, and send the photo via Telegram. Never type the code into any public website — some sites log entered codes before submission.
